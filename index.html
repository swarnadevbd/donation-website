Bangladesh has been hit with the worst flooding in 100 years. Thousands of children are at risk, threatened by a high risk of drowning, disease, and malnutrition. More than 36,000 children are crowded into emergency flood shelters.


Families and children are trapped at home waiting for boats to bring relief. Roads and homes have been submerged, power stations are down, and over 380 schools have been closed or impacted.

How to Contribute?

Give today to the
UNICEF emergency response

Your donation can help UNICEF supply water and sanitation kits, including soap, water purification tablets, jerry cans, sanitary napkins, and buckets, to ensure that children and their families have access to clean water and hygiene items during and after the floods.


Your donation also helps to ensure continued education for children, providing learning support like Emergency Education Kits for 6-to-10-year-olds so they can keep studying.
